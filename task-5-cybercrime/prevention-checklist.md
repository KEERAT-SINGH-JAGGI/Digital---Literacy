# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 5 | Keerat Singh Jaggi | 25BAI10636**

> A practical guide for college students in India to stay safe from UPI fraud,
> phishing, and other online threats.

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — never reuse the same password
      across email, UPI apps, and social media.

- [ ] **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts including
      Gmail, Instagram, and banking apps.

- [ ] **Never click on links received via SMS or WhatsApp** that ask you to log in,
      verify your account, or claim a prize. Always go directly to the official website.

- [ ] **Check URLs carefully before entering any credentials** — fake sites often use
      slight misspellings like "paytrn.com" instead of "paytm.com".

- [ ] **Keep your phone's OS and apps updated** — security patches fix vulnerabilities
      that fraudsters actively exploit.

- [ ] **Do not install apps from unknown sources or APK links** shared via WhatsApp or
      Telegram, even from people you know.

- [ ] **Review app permissions regularly** — a calculator app should never need access
      to your contacts, camera, or SMS.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety (Specific Tips)

- [ ] **Remember: You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.**
      Any instruction to enter your PIN in order to get a refund or payment is a scam —
      period. Your PIN only authorises outgoing payments.

- [ ] **Always read the full UPI screen before confirming** — distinguish between
      "Pay" (you send money) and "Collect" (someone requests money from you).
      Fraudsters send collect requests disguised as refunds.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app to minimise potential
      losses if you are ever deceived.

- [ ] **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV** with anyone over the phone — not even
      someone claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay support, or a government office.

- [ ] **Verify buyers/sellers independently** before any transaction on OLX, Facebook
      Marketplace, or Quikr — search their phone number online for fraud reports.

---

## Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Keep personal information private** — avoid posting your phone number, home
      address, or daily schedule publicly.

- [ ] **Be sceptical of "too good to be true" offers** — fake scholarship schemes,
      free iPhone giveaways, and work-from-home jobs with huge salaries are common
      traps targeting students.

- [ ] **Do not accept friend/follow requests from unknown accounts** — especially those
      with few posts, no profile photo, or a recently created account.

---

## If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Helpline** | Call **1930** (24x7, all days) |
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | File a complaint at **cybercrime.gov.in** |
| **Your Bank** | Call your bank's fraud helpline immediately to freeze the transaction |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest cyber crime cell |

> **Act within the first hour.** UPI fraud complaints filed quickly have a higher chance
> of a transaction hold before funds are withdrawn by the fraudster.
